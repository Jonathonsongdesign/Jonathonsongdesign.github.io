Seeker

Project Infomation

Genre: 3D platform /Puzzle/ Story Game
Platform: PC
Software&Language: Unreal Engine, Blueprint , Adobe PS, AE, PR
Duration: Three days
Independent development: script writing, game design, level design, scene building, programming, video making


Introduction

“In case What you are looking for is a light year away from you.Do you know what to do?Are you going to give up?”

People always realized what they once had after they lost it. In fact,you know what you have, and you just never thought you’d lose them.

“Seeker” is a philosophical and emotional experience game that expresses the abstract emotions about losing and gaining.

[Core Game]: Players move around by W,S,A,D in order to solve several puzzles, causing changes in time and space and everything. Through time and space, they constantly search for and collect the pieces of the “treasures” that they are eager to find. Players collect the four pieces that are left behind in order to trigger the end of the complete story.

[Game Features]: Each chapter of the game will present a new image of time and space with different art designs. Each chapter which is based on real history and philosophy tells a philosophical story. The currently completed chapters are: Berkeley Space, Hanguguan Pass, The Great Wall and modern city.

Motivation of creation:

From July 16th to July 18th, 2018, I was fortunate enough to be selected by Tencent NEXTIDEA Studio to participate in Tencent NEXTIDEA’s first game development camp.
The theme of the development camp was “See Traditional Culture from the future”, which required inspiration from the Historical monument, terracotta warriors and horses of Xi’an.

Live picture：


I always love to reflect on life and game design is my favorite way to express all my new ideas and emotion. It feels really amazing to say what I want to express through the game. That is why I am so interested in game design, just like this demo I produced in my sophomore year in Tecent NEXTIDEA studio. I have previously read an article on the Berkeley philosophy on Google, which has a sentence full of philosophical thoughts: “The table I write on, I say, exists, that is, I see and feel it; And if I were out of my study I should say it existed, meaning that that if I was in my study I might perceive it, or that some other spirit actually does perceive it. (PHK 3)”

[From Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, George Berkeley, First published Fri Sep 10, 2004; substantive revision Wed Jan 19, 2011, Chapter 3.2.4, Unperceived objects—Principles vs. Dialogues,the website:https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/berkeley/#3.2.2].

I understood his philosophical relationship between seeing and fantasy. Seeing means what we have in our lives or what we get while fantasy is what we have never got in our lives or what we have lost. So I figured out how to use the game to make it easy for players to understand the relationship of this link of philosophy (the relationship between getting and losing). In the end, I decided to make a philosophical and emotional experience game in order to tell everyone abstract emotions about losing and getting.
